In spherical coordinates we have

\begin{align*} \nabla \cdot \mathbf{D} & = \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial \left ( r^2 D_{r} \right )}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r \sin \theta} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} \left ( D_{\theta} \sin \theta \right ) + \frac{1}{r \sin \theta} \frac{\partial D_{\phi}}{\partial \phi} \\ & = \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial \left ( r^2 D_{r} \right )}{\partial r} + 0 + 0 \\ & = \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial }{\partial r} \frac{Q r^2}{4 \pi r^2} \\ & = 0 \\ \end{align*}

For \(r \ne 0\). Taken in the limit \(\lim _{r \rightarrow 0}\):

\begin{align*} \lim _{r \rightarrow 0} \nabla \cdot \mathbf{D} & = \lim _{r \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial }{\partial r} \frac{Q r^2}{4 \pi r^2} = \infty \end{align*}
