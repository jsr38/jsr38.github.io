1 Weather satellite APT

What's all this for? We want to receive weather satellite transmissions. The source for detailed information I've used is http://noaasis.noaa.gov/NOAASIS/pubs/Users_Guide-Building_Receive_Stations_March_2009.pdf

.

The currently operating Polar Operational Environmental Satellites POES are NOAA-15, NOAA-18 and NOAA-19 and operational status is found at http://www.ospo.noaa.gov/Operations/POES/status.html . These provide APT transmissions in the VHF at around 137MHz and HRPT on S-band. The geostationary satellites currently operating are GOES-13 and GOES-15 (GOES-14 is in a storage orbit) which provide GVAR and LRIT services on S-band.

The gpredict http://gpredict.oz9aec.net/ tool provides an extremely useful way of tracking these satellites.

2 Receiver characteristics

The official documentation noaaug provides information and guidance on receiver characteristics.

2.1 Receiver frequency

Documentation for POES states carrier deviation of \(\pm 17 \)kHz and doppler shift during a direct overhead pass of \(\pm 4.5\)kHz which yields a maximum carrier deviation of \(\pm 21.5 \)kHz.

The frequencies of interest are 137.50, 137.62, 137.1, 137.9125 MHz inside the allocated band of 137-138MHz.
